Most career advice talks at you. This one lets you talk to yourself — a grounded, ten-years-older version that remembers where you started, sounds like an evolution of how you already speak, and describes the life you're heading toward in concrete scenes rather than abstractions.

The session

Four steps, one sitting.

Everything happens in the browser, in a single ~20–30 minute visit.

01

Warm‑up

A brief questionnaire built from established psychological measures — your personality, what you value in work, the activities that pull you in, and how settled your career feels right now.

02

Find a direction

A guide reads your profile and proposes a handful of concrete career paths, each framed for the next decade. You pick the one you want to step into.

03

Meet your future self

Ten years on, that version of you talks with you about the life you built — vivid, specific, and traceably continuous with who you are today.

04

Reflect

A closing questionnaire captures what, if anything, shifted — how close and how confident you feel about where you're headed.

The research

A small, careful pilot.

This is a controlled study. Participants are randomly assigned across a few variants of the experience, and the same validated measures are taken before and after — so we can look at whether a personal, episodic conversation with one's future self moves how people feel about their direction.

Findings are treated as an early, descriptive pilot; the sample is intentionally small.

Design
Pre / post, randomised across conditions
Measures
Personality, work values, vocational interests, career confidence, self‑continuity & closeness
Format
Single browser session · self‑paced chat
Data
Stored pseudonymously, for research only
This page is intentionally high‑level. If you may take part, that's all you need — the experience speaks for itself, and we keep the specifics light so they don't colour how you go through it.
Under the hood

Built to be measured.

A precompiled React frontend with no bundler, an Express backend, and Postgres persistence. A gated researcher dashboard manages sessions and de‑identified exports, and an LLM‑as‑judge pipeline measures how faithfully the system reproduces real participants' ratings.
